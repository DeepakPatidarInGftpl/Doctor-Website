Frozen Shoulder (Adhesive Capsulitis):
  • Definition: A condition characterized by the gradual onset of shoulder pain and stiffness, leading to decreased range of motion.
  • Causes: The exact cause is often unknown, but it may be associated with inflammation, injury, or underlying medical conditions.
  • Stages: Typically progresses through three stages - painful, frozen (stiffness predominates), and thawing (gradual improvement).

Physiotherapy Management:

  • Pain Management: Modalities like heat or cold therapy, and exercises to relieve pain.
  • Range of Motion (ROM) Exercises: Gentle stretching to improve flexibility and prevent further stiffness.
  • Strengthening Exercises: Gradual introduction of strengthening exercises for the shoulder muscles.
  • Joint Mobilization: Manual techniques to improve joint mobility.
  • Home Exercise Program: Prescribing exercises for the patient to perform regularly at home.

Shoulder Capsulitis (Capsulitis Adhaesiva or Adhesive Capsulitis):

  • Definition: Inflammation and thickening of the shoulder joint capsule, leading to pain and restricted movement.
  • Causes: Often idiopathic, but can be associated with trauma, surgery, or underlying medical conditions.

Physiotherapy Management:

  • Pain Relief: Modalities like heat or cold therapy for pain management.
  • Stretching Exercises: Gentle and progressive stretches to improve flexibility.
  • Joint Mobilization: Manual techniques to address stiffness and improve joint mobility.
  • Strengthening ExercisesGradual introduction of strengthening exercises, focusing on the rotator cuff and shoulder muscles.
  • Functional Activities: Incorporating activities that mimic daily tasks to improve overall function.
  • Patient Education: Informing the patient about the condition, the importance of compliance with exercises, and home-based management.

General Considerations for Both Conditions:

  • Individualized Treatment: Tailoring the physiotherapy program to the specific needs and stage of the condition.
  • Consistency: Regular and consistent performance of prescribed exercises and stretches.
  • Patience: Both conditions may take time to resolve, and patience is essential for gradual improvement.
  • Communication: Open communication with the physiotherapist to address concerns and modify the treatment plan as needed.

It's crucial for individuals with frozen shoulder or shoulder capsulitis to consult with a healthcare professional, including a physiotherapist, for a comprehensive assessment and personalized treatment plan. Physiotherapy is a key component in managing these conditions, aiming to reduce pain, improve range of motion, and enhance overall shoulder function.

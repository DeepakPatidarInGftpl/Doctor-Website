LASER THERAPY

Laser therapy, also known as low-level laser therapy (LLLT) or photobiomodulation, is a form of treatment that uses low-level lasers or light-emitting diodes (LEDs) to stimulate cellular function. In the context of physiotherapy, laser therapy is employed as a non-invasive modality to promote healing, reduce pain, and manage various musculoskeletal conditions. Here are some key aspects of laser therapy in physiotherapy:


  • 1. Mechanism of Action:
    • Laser therapy is believed to work at the cellular level, where photons of light are absorbed by the mitochondria, leading to increased ATP (adenosine triphosphate) production.
    • It involves applying low-voltage electrical currents through electrodes placed on the skin over the painful area
    • This increased energy production is thought to enhance cellular function, promote tissue repair, and reduce inflammation
  • 2. Pain Management:
    • Laser therapy is often used in physiotherapy for pain management. It may help reduce pain by inhibiting nerve signals and modulating inflammation.
    • Conditions such as arthritis, tendonitis, and other chronic pain conditions may benefit from laser therapy
  • 3. Tissue Repair and Regeneration:
    • Laser therapy is thought to accelerate the healing process by promoting cell proliferation and tissue regeneration.
    • It may be used in the rehabilitation of injuries, including strains, sprains, and soft tissue injuries.
  • 4. Anti-Inflammatory Effects:
    • The therapy is believed to have anti-inflammatory effects by reducing the production of inflammatory mediators and promoting the resolution of inflammation.
    • This can be beneficial in conditions characterized by inflammation, such as arthritis.
  • 5. Muscle Relaxation:
    • Laser therapy may help relax muscles and reduce muscle spasms, making it useful in managing conditions associated with muscle tightness.
  • 6. Wound Healing:
    • Physiotherapists may use laser therapy to facilitate wound healing in cases of chronic wounds or surgical incisions.
    • It can stimulate the production of collagen, an essential component in the healing process.
  • 7. Non-Invasive Treatment:
    • Laser therapy is non-invasive and generally well-tolerated, making it a suitable option for individuals seeking alternatives to more invasive treatments.

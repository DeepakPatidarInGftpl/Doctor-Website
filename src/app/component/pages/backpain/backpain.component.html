BACK PAIN

Back pain can have various causes, and treatment may depend on the specific issue. Here are some common causes and general suggestions:

  • Muscle Strain: Overexertion or improper lifting can strain the muscles in your back. Rest, gentle stretching, and over-the-counter pain medications may help.
  • Herniated Disc: This occurs when the soft tissue inside the discs between your vertebrae pushes out. Treatment may involve physical therapy, medications, or in severe cases, surgery.
  • Sciatica: Compression or irritation of the sciatic nerve can cause pain that radiates down the leg. Treatment may include physical therapy, medications, or in some cases, surgery.
  • Structural Issues: Scoliosis, spinal stenosis, or other structural problems can contribute to back pain. Treatment will depend on the underlying cause and may include physical therapy, medications, or surgery.
  • Lifestyle Factors: Lack of exercise, obesity, and smoking can contribute to back pain. Adopting a healthy lifestyle, including regular exercise and maintaining a healthy weight, can be beneficial.

If you're experiencing persistent or severe back pain, it's important to seek medical attention. Your healthcare provider can perform a thorough examination, order diagnostic tests if necessary, and recommend an appropriate treatment plan based on the underlying cause of your back pain.

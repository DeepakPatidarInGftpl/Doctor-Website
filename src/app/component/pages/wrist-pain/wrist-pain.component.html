Wrist Pain
  • Wrist pain can result from various causes, including injuries, overuse, or medical conditions. Here are some common types and causes of wrist pain and general suggestions for managing it:
1. De Quervain's Tenosynovitis:
  • Description: Inflammation of the tendons on the thumb side of the wrist.
  • Symptoms: Pain and swelling at the base of the thumb, difficulty gripping or forming a fist.
    • Carpal Tunnel Syndrome:
2.Carpal Tunnel Syndrome:
  • Description: Inflammation of the tendons on the thumb side of the wrist.
  • Symptoms: Pain and swelling at the base of the thumb, difficulty gripping or forming a fist.
    • Carpal Tunnel Syndrome:
3.Wrist Sprain:
  • Description: Stretching or tearing of ligaments in the wrist.
  • Symptoms:Pain, swelling, and difficulty moving the wrist
4.TFCC (Triangular Fibrocartilage Complex) Injury:
  • Description: Injury to the cartilage and ligaments in the wrist.
  • Symptoms:Pain on the pinky side of the wrist, clicking or popping sensations
1. Pain Management:
  • Modalities: Use of heat or cold therapy to manage pain and inflammation.
  • Symptoms: Pain and swelling at the base of the thumb, difficulty gripping or forming a fist.
    • Carpal Tunnel Syndrome:
2. Range of Motion (ROM) Exercises:
  • Gentle Stretching: Controlled stretching exercises to improve flexibility.
  • Active and Passive ROM Exercises: Movements Movements to maintain or restore normal joint function.
    • Carpal Tunnel Syndrome:
3. Strengthening Exercises
  • Isometric Exercises: Exercises without joint movement to strengthen wrist muscles.
  • Isometric Exercises: Exercises without joint movement to strengthen wrist muscles.
4. Splinting and Bracing:
  • Orthotic Devices: Custom or prefabricated splints to support and immobilize the wrist during healing.
  • Wrist Braces:For conditions like De Quervain's tenosynovitis, providing support to the thumb and wrist.

It's crucial for individuals with frozen shoulder or shoulder capsulitis to consult with a healthcare professional, including a physiotherapist, for a comprehensive assessment and personalized treatment plan.
